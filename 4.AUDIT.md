## Purpose of This Document

This document describes the **externally auditable surface**
of the repository bundle.

It answers exactly one question:

> **How can one verify that this system
> adheres to its own stated rules?**

Audit here means:
- traceability
- reproducibility
- rule compliance
- transparency of limits

Auditability does **not** imply
unrestricted, complete, or automated access
to all data or artifacts.

---

## 1. Audit Goals (Binding)

An audit serves to demonstrate that:

- no implicit truth decisions are made
- epistemic, operative, and instantiational layers are strictly separated
- artifacts are versioned and referencable
- provenance is explicitly documented
- known limitations are openly declared

An audit does **not** serve:
- content evaluation
- conflict resolution
- normative assessment
- authority validation

## 6. Scope Delimitation

This audit system is **not**:

- a certification authority
- a truth arbiter
- a governance institution
- a quality seal
- a content validation mechanism

It audits **structural integrity**, not correctness.
