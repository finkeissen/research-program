# SELF-APPLICATION
## Münchhausen, Self-Justification, and Structural Self-Plausibility

---

## Status

This document is **non-normative**.

It introduces no new rules,
modifies no admissibility conditions,
and binds no downstream system.

Its sole function is to expose and test
the **self-application** of the problem-atom schema.

---

## Motivation (Explicit)

Any system that defines admissibility conditions
faces a classical risk:

> It may exempt itself from the rules it imposes.

This document does not address this risk
through external justification,
meta-theory,
or normative argumentation.

Instead, it performs an explicit **Münchhausen move**:

> The structure is applied to itself
> without granting itself special status.

This is referred to here as **self-plausibility**,
not validation or proof.

---

## Self-Application as a Problem Atom

The problem-atom schema itself
is treated here **as a problem atom**.

No exception is introduced.

---

### Problem

Existence and admissibility of the problem-atom schema itself
within an epistemic architecture that prohibits implicit authority.

---

### Symptom

The schema introduces binding admissibility constraints
while remaining structurally external
to its own instantiation rules.

---

### Cause  
*(epistemic status: assumed)*

Any admissibility system risks implicit authority
if it is not itself subject
to the conditions it enforces.

---

### Consequence

Unacknowledged meta-authority
at the schema level.

Loss of contestability
through structural exemption.

---

### Relational Closure

This self-application introduces
no external references,
no hidden containers,
and no auxiliary structures.

The schema refers only to itself
under its own admissibility constraints.

---

### Epistemic Status

---

### Temporal Admissibility

### Freedom / Binding Tension
